Viola Davis

Here's a rough time line of Viola's life:

  1. Born in St. Matthews, South Carolina
  2. Graduates from Rhode Island College as a theater major
  3. Attends Juilliard School and is a member of the school's Drama Division "Group 22".
  4. Stars in an off Broadway production of William Shakespeare's comedy 'As You Like It' as Denis
  5. Makes her Broadway debut in the original Broadway production of August Wilson's Seven Guitars as Vera
  6. Receives Screen Actors Guild card
  7. Returns to the Broadway stage in another play by August Wilson titled King Hedley II as Tonya
  8. Wins her first Tony Award for Best Featured Actress in a Play and a Drama Desk Award
  9. Wins another Drama Desk Award for her work in a 2004 off-Broadway production called Intimate Apparel by Lynn Nottage
  10. Plays Mrs Miller in the film adaptation of the Broadway play Doubt by Patrick Shanley
  11. Nominated for several awards including a Screen Actors Guild Award, a Golden Globe Award and an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress
  12. Inducted into the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences
  13. Returns to Broadway play, a revival of Fences as Rose Maxson
  14. Wins her second Tony Award becoming the second African-American to win the Tony for Best Perfomance by a Leading Actress in a Play after Phylicia Rashad
  15. Stars as Aibileen Clark, a housemaid in 1960s Mississipi, in the film adaptation of Kathryn Stockett's novel The Help
  16. Wins two Screen Actors Guild Awards in addition to being nominated for the Academy Award, Golden Globe Award and the BAFTA Award
  17. listed as one of the most influential people in the world by Time magazine
  18. Glamour magazine names Davis Glamour's Film Actress of the year
  19. Wins the Women in Film's Crystal Award
  20. Gets cast in How to Get Away with Murder produced by Shonda Rhimes
  21. Becomes the first African-American to win the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress for her role as Annalise Keating
  22. Gets second nomination for the Primetime Emmy Award
  23. Wins two Screen Actors Guild Awards for Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor
